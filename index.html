Regional Trends in the Misrepresentation of Illicit Drugs

Volunteer work as an analyst for the harm reduction charity Erowid.


Illicit substances are commonly misrepresented to increases profit for dealers. This practice is widespread and extremely dangerous. Understanding the traits of this practice is crucial to maintaining public safety. I am volunteering with Erowid Center to analyze and interpret their data to find regional trends in the misrepresentation of illicit substances. This information is useful for care workers, medical staff, law makers and law enforcement.

EcstasyData.org is an independent laboratory pill testing program run by Erowid Center with support from Isomer Design and Dancesafe. With over four thousand pills analyzed from all over the world, this data set is too large and complex to be analysed by hand.

I am currently cleaning the data set in preparation to run cluster analysis on it and generate a self-organizing map.

Refining AI Black-Boxes with Reinforcement Learning

A current passion project where I test the capability to refine AI performance with neural nets.


In this simple game, called Monkey Want Banana, the player controls a monkey to search for bananas on a game board while traversing an environment of barriers, lava and other monkeys. After writing a simple AI to play the game, a neural net is trained off of labelled data generated by the AI. When the neural net accurately reproduces the AI behavior, it is allowed to continue training in the game to refine the AI's algorithm via reinforcement learning. The most interesting aspect of this is that the method treats the AI as a black-box, so any arbitrary AI can be used.

Upgrading Bitumen with Lasers

This project was completed during a six-month contract at the University of Calgary.


One of the products from Alberta's oil sands is bitumen, a extremely viscous hydrocarbon source with heavy, branching organic compounds. In this state it is not usable for fuel, and is difficult to transport. There are many methods of upgrading bitumen into crude oil, a lighter hydrocarbon that is more easily transported and later refined into petroleum products like gasoline. In this project, I lead the development of a novel technique for upgrading bitumen using lasers.

Quantum-Correlated Twin beams in OPO for High-Resolution Spectroscopy in the Rapid Scan Mode

This project was completed as part of a student summer-research scholarship.


Optical Parametric Oscillators (OPOs) are used extensively in molecular spectroscopy. Maximally Entangled Multipartite Symmetric States

This was my thesis project for my BSc. in Physics.


Bosons are one of two types of fundamental particles in physics. When you have more than one identical boson (such as two protons) they must exist in a symmetric state.

There is currently a great deal of research on maximizing entanglement in a system where it can be used as a resource. Applications of this exist in fields such as quantum computing and quantum cryptography. This naturally leads to my project focusing on maximizing entanglement in systems of identical bosons (symmetric states).

During this project, I found an upper limit on the entanglement of a symmetric state, and found several states that reached this upper limit in two to seven level systems.

Gibbs Volume Entropy

This was an independent research project on Gibbs' of volume entropy contrasting it with the common formulation of Boltzmann entropy.


Josiah Willard Gibbs and Ludwig Boltzman were two extremely influential physicists that did a great deal of work in the field of statistical mechanics. In particular, they both worked to formalized the emergent phenomenon of entropy from thermodynamics into a rigorous reductionist model motivated by the basic laws of physics.

In this project, I researched the history of both of these fomulations for entropy, and derived some interesting ramifications related to thermal equilibrium and negative temperature.
